# APPENDIX B: SAFETY DATA SHEETS (SDS)
## Product/Material Supply - Safety Data Sheets

### 1. GENERAL REQUIREMENTS

#### 1.1 Scope
This appendix contains Safety Data Sheets (SDS) for all products/materials supplied under this Purchase Order. SDS are provided in accordance with:
- **GHS (Globally Harmonized System)** for classification and labeling
- **OSHA Hazard Communication Standard** 29 CFR 1910.1200
- **REACH Regulation (EC) No 1907/2006**
- **CLP Regulation (EC) No 1272/2008**

#### 1.2 SDS Requirements
Each SDS must contain 16 sections as per GHS requirements:
1. Identification
2. Hazard identification
3. Composition/information on ingredients
4. First-aid measures
5. Fire-fighting measures
6. Accidental release measures
7. Handling and storage
8. Exposure controls/personal protection
9. Physical and chemical properties
10. Stability and reactivity
11. Toxicological information
12. Ecological information
13. Disposal considerations
14. Transport information
15. Regulatory information
16. Other information

#### 2.4 Section 4: First-Aid Measures
**Eye Contact:** Flush with plenty of water for 15 minutes. Seek medical attention if irritation persists.
**Skin Contact:** Wash with soap and water. No specific treatment required.
**Inhalation:** Remove to fresh air. Seek medical attention if symptoms develop.
**Ingestion:** Do not induce vomiting. Seek immediate medical attention.

#### 2.5 Section 5: Fire-Fighting Measures
**Extinguishing Media:** Foam, CO₂, dry chemical, water spray
**Special Hazards:** Combustion produces toxic gases (CO, CO₂, SO₂)
**Protective Equipment:** Self-contained breathing apparatus
**Fire Fighting Instructions:** Cool containers with water spray

#### 2.6 Section 6: Accidental Release Measures
**Personal Precautions:** Wear appropriate PPE
**Environmental Precautions:** Prevent entry into drains and watercourses
**Containment/Cleanup:** Absorb with inert material and dispose as waste oil
**Reporting:** Report spills to environmental authorities if required

#### 2.7 Section 7: Handling and Storage
**Handling:** Use good industrial hygiene practices
**Storage:** Store in cool, dry place away from heat sources
**Incompatibilities:** Strong oxidizing agents
**Shelf Life:** 5 years from date of manufacture

#### 2.8 Section 8: Exposure Controls/Personal Protection
**Engineering Controls:** Local exhaust ventilation if mists are generated
**PPE Requirements:**
- **Eyes:** Safety glasses
- **Skin:** Gloves, protective clothing if prolonged contact
- **Respiratory:** None required under normal use

**Exposure Limits:** No specific occupational exposure limits established

#### 2.9 Section 9: Physical and Chemical Properties
**Appearance:** Amber colored liquid
**Odor:** Mild petroleum odor
**pH:** Not applicable
**Boiling Point:** >300°C
**Flash Point:** >200°C
**Viscosity:** 15W-40 grade
**Solubility:** Insoluble in water

### 5. HAZARD COMMUNICATION

#### 5.1 Label Requirements
All lubricant containers must be labeled with:
- Product name and code
- Supplier contact information
- Hazard classification (if applicable)
- Safety precautions
- Batch number and date

#### 5.2 Training Requirements
Personnel handling lubricants must be trained in:
- Safe handling procedures
- Spill response
- PPE usage
- Emergency procedures
- Waste disposal

### 6. EMERGENCY RESPONSE

#### 6.1 Spill Response
**Small Spills (<25L):**
- Contain spill with absorbent material
- Clean up with appropriate absorbents
- Dispose as hazardous waste if required

**Large Spills (>25L):**
- Evacuate area
- Contact emergency services
- Contain and prevent spread
- Professional cleanup required

#### 6.2 Fire Response
- Use appropriate extinguishing media
- Wear self-contained breathing apparatus
- Cool containers to prevent rupture
- Prevent runoff into drains

#### 6.3 Medical Emergency
- Contact poison control center
- Provide SDS to medical personnel
- Transport to nearest medical facility
- Report incident to supplier
