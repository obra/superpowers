# TESTING PROCEDURES SPECIFICATION
## Construction Specification Standards - Quality Assurance & Testing

### 1. GENERAL REQUIREMENTS

#### 1.1 Scope
This specification covers the testing procedures and quality assurance requirements for all construction materials, workmanship, and finished products for the construction project.

#### 1.2 References
- SANS 3001: Civil Engineering Test Methods
- TMH1: Standard Methods for Testing Road Construction Materials
- BS EN 12350: Testing Fresh Concrete
- BS EN 12390: Testing Hardened Concrete
- BS EN 10002: Tensile Testing of Metallic Materials
- ASTM Standards for Construction Testing

### 2. SAMPLING PROCEDURES

#### 2.1 Concrete Sampling
**Fresh Concrete Sampling:**
- Method: BS EN 12350-1
- Sample size: Minimum 4 liters per sample
- Sampling frequency: Every 20m³ or per truck, whichever greater
- Transport: Within 15 minutes to laboratory

**Hardened Concrete Sampling:**
- Method: BS EN 12390-1
- Core diameter: 100mm minimum
- Core length: 2×diameter minimum
- Storage: Cured in water at 20±2°C

#### 2.2 Soil Sampling
**Disturbed Samples:**
- Method: BS 1377-1
- Sample size: 1kg minimum
- Containers: Sealed plastic bags
- Preservation: Cool, dark storage

**Undisturbed Samples:**
- Method: BS 1377-2
- Tube diameter: 100mm
- Tube length: 200mm
- Preservation: Wax sealed ends

#### 2.3 Steel Sampling
**Reinforcement Steel:**
- Method: BS EN 10002-1
- Sample length: 500mm minimum
- Number of samples: 3 per batch
- Identification: Batch number and heat number

**Structural Steel:**
- Method: BS EN 10002-1
- Sample location: End of each member
- Test pieces: Full section thickness
- Documentation: Mill certificates required

### 3. CONCRETE TESTING PROCEDURES

#### 3.1 Fresh Concrete Tests
**Slump Test:**
- Method: SANS 5861-3
- Frequency: Every truck delivery
- Acceptance: Within ±25mm of specified slump
- Reporting: Actual slump value and time

**Air Content Test:**
- Method: SANS 5861-4 (Pressure method)
- Frequency: Every 50m³ or per mix change
- Acceptance: Within ±1.5% of specified air content
- Equipment: Type A air meter

**Concrete Temperature:**
- Method: ASTM C1064
- Frequency: Each delivery
- Acceptance: 5°C to 32°C
- Equipment: Digital thermometer

#### 3.2 Hardened Concrete Tests
**Compressive Strength:**
- Method: SANS 5861-1 (150mm cubes)
- Curing: 7 days and 28 days at 20±2°C
- Frequency: 3 cubes per 30m³
- Acceptance: Average ≥ specified strength, no cube <85% of specified

**Cube Making and Curing:**
- Method: SANS 5861-2
- Moulds: Steel or plastic, 150mm
- Curing: Water tank at 20±2°C
- Demoulding: After 24±2 hours

**Core Testing:**
- Method: SANS 5861-6
- Diameter: 100mm minimum
- Length: 2×diameter minimum
- Acceptance: 85% of specified strength minimum

### 4. SOIL AND GEOTECHNICAL TESTING

#### 4.1 Compaction Testing
**Modified AASHTO Test:**
- Method: TMH1 Method A7
- Sample preparation: 5kg of material
- Compaction: 56 blows per layer, 5 layers
- Reporting: Optimum moisture content and maximum dry density

**Field Density Testing:**
- Method: Nuclear gauge (ASTM D6938)
- Calibration: Daily calibration check
- Frequency: One test per 500m²
- Acceptance: 95% of laboratory maximum dry density

#### 4.2 California Bearing Ratio (CBR)
**Laboratory CBR:**
- Method: TMH1 Method A12
- Sample preparation: 5kg disturbed sample
- Soaking: 4 days minimum
- Reporting: CBR at 2.5mm and 5.0mm penetration

**In Situ CBR:**
- Method: Dynamic cone penetrometer (DCP)
- Frequency: One test per 1000m²
- Correlation: Convert to CBR using approved charts

#### 4.3 Atterberg Limits
**Liquid Limit:**
- Method: TMH1 Method A2
- Frequency: One test per material source
- Equipment: Casagrande apparatus
- Reporting: Liquid limit and flow curve

**Plastic Limit:**
- Method: TMH1 Method A2
- Frequency: One test per material source
- Procedure: Rolling thread method
- Reporting: Plastic limit value

### 5. STEEL TESTING PROCEDURES

#### 5.1 Reinforcement Steel Testing
**Tensile Testing:**
- Method: BS EN 10002-1
- Sample preparation: Machined to standard dimensions
- Strain rate: 0.00025/s until yield, then 0.005/s
- Reporting: Yield strength, ultimate strength, elongation

**Bend Testing:**
- Method: BS EN 10002-1
- Mandrel diameter: 4×bar diameter
- Angle: 180° bend
- Acceptance: No cracks visible to naked eye

**Chemical Analysis:**
- Method: Optical emission spectroscopy
- Elements: C, Mn, P, S, Si, Cu, Ni, Cr
- Frequency: One test per heat number
- Acceptance: Within SANS 920 specifications

#### 5.2 Structural Steel Testing
**Charpy Impact Testing:**
- Method: BS EN 10045-1
- Temperature: -20°C for S355, -40°C for S460
- Sample size: 10×10mm cross-section
- Acceptance: 27J minimum average, 19J minimum single value

**Ultrasonic Testing:**
- Method: BS EN 10160
- Equipment: Straight beam probes
- Acceptance: No defects exceeding quality level B
- Reporting: Defect location, size, and type

### 6. ASPHALT AND BITUMINOUS TESTING

#### 6.1 Binder Testing
**Penetration Test:**
- Method: TMH1 Method A10
- Temperature: 25°C
- Load: 100g for 5 seconds
- Reporting: Penetration in 0.1mm units

**Softening Point:**
- Method: TMH1 Method A11 (Ring and ball)
- Heating rate: 5°C/min
- Reporting: Softening point temperature

**Viscosity Testing:**
- Method: TMH1 Method A9 (Brookfield viscometer)
- Temperature: 135°C for mixing, 120°C for compaction
- Reporting: Viscosity in Pa·s

#### 6.2 Asphalt Mix Testing
**Marshall Stability:**
- Method: TMH1 Method A14
- Sample preparation: 1200g mix
- Compaction: 75 blows per face
- Testing: At 60°C
- Reporting: Stability (kN) and flow (mm)

**Bulk Density:**
- Method: TMH1 Method A15
- Sample: Marshall briquette
- Procedure: Saturated surface dry method
- Reporting: Bulk density and voids

### 7. AGGREGATE TESTING PROCEDURES

#### 7.1 Particle Size Distribution
**Sieve Analysis:**
- Method: TMH1 Method A1
- Sample size: 5kg for coarse, 1kg for fine
- Sieves: BS series sieves
- Reporting: Percentage passing each sieve size

**Fineness Modulus:**
- Calculation: Sum of cumulative percentages divided by 100
- Coarse aggregate: 5.5-8.0
- Fine aggregate: 2.0-3.5

#### 7.2 Aggregate Strength Tests
**Aggregate Crushing Value:**
- Method: TMH1 Method A4
- Sample: 10-14mm aggregate
- Load: 400kN
- Acceptance: Maximum 25% for base course

**10% Fines Value:**
- Method: TMH1 Method A5
- Sample: 10-14mm aggregate
- Load: To produce 10% fines
- Acceptance: Minimum 100kN

#### 7.3 Aggregate Durability Tests
**Los Angeles Abrasion:**
- Method: TMH1 Method A3
- Sample: 5kg of 10-14mm aggregate
- Charges: 12 steel spheres
- Acceptance: Maximum 30% loss

**Soundness Test:**
- Method: TMH1 Method A6
- Solution: Magnesium sulfate
- Cycles: 5 cycles
- Acceptance: Maximum 12% loss

### 8. NON-DESTRUCTIVE TESTING

#### 8.1 Concrete Testing
**Rebound Hammer:**
- Method: BS EN 12504-2
- Calibration: Daily on concrete test anvil
- Frequency: 10 readings per location
- Correlation: Established correlation curve required

**Ultrasonic Pulse Velocity:**
- Method: BS EN 12504-4
- Path length: 150mm minimum
- Frequency: 54kHz
- Reporting: Pulse velocity and estimated strength

#### 8.2 Steel Testing
**Magnetic Particle Inspection:**
- Method: BS EN 10246-11
- Current: AC for surface, DC for subsurface
- Medium: White contrast paint, black magnetic ink
- Acceptance: No relevant indications

**Dye Penetrant Testing:**
- Method: BS EN 10246-12
- Cleaner: Solvent removable
- Penetrant: Fluorescent or visible
- Acceptance: No relevant indications

### 9. ENVIRONMENTAL TESTING

#### 9.1 Water Quality Testing
**pH Testing:**
- Method: pH meter calibration
- Frequency: Daily
- Acceptance: 6.0-8.5 for mixing water

**Chloride Content:**
- Method: Ion chromatography
- Frequency: Weekly
- Acceptance: Maximum 500ppm

**Sulfate Content:**
- Method: Turbidimetric method
- Frequency: Weekly
- Acceptance: Maximum 1000ppm

#### 9.2 Air Quality Testing
**Dust Monitoring:**
- Method: Gravimetric analysis
- Frequency: Daily during earthworks
- Acceptance: PM10 <75μg/m³, PM2.5 <35μg/m³

**Noise Monitoring:**
- Method: Sound level meter
- Frequency: Weekly
- Acceptance: L10 <70dB during construction

### 10. QUALITY ASSURANCE PROCEDURES

#### 10.1 Test Reporting
**Test Certificates:**
- Format: Standardized laboratory report
- Content: Test method, results, acceptance criteria
- Distribution: Engineer, contractor, client
- Retention: Minimum 5 years

**Non-Conformance Reports:**
- Format: NCR form with corrective actions
- Distribution: All relevant parties
- Follow-up: Verification of corrective actions
- Closure: Engineer approval required

#### 10.2 Statistical Quality Control
**Control Charts:**
- Type: X-bar and R charts for key parameters
- Parameters: Concrete strength, density, layer thickness
- Action limits: ±3 standard deviations
- Review: Weekly by quality manager

**Process Capability:**
- Calculation: Cp = (USL-LSL)/(6×σ)
- Target: Cp ≥ 1.33 for critical parameters
- Review: Monthly quality review meetings

### 11. LABORATORY ACCREDITATION

#### 11.1 Accreditation Requirements
**SANAS Accreditation:**
- Scope: Relevant test methods
- Level: Testing laboratory accreditation
- Surveillance: Annual assessments
- Certificates: Current and valid

**Internal Quality Control:**
- Equipment calibration: Traceable to national standards
- Proficiency testing: Annual participation
- Method validation: Regular verification
- Staff competency: Annual training

#### 11.2 Equipment Maintenance
**Daily Checks:**
- Balances: Calibration with standard weights
- Thermometers: Ice point check
- Sieves: Visual inspection for damage
- Moulds: Dimensional verification

**Monthly Maintenance:**
- Compression machines: Load verification
- Ovens: Temperature uniformity
- Refrigerators: Temperature monitoring
- pH meters: Buffer solution calibration

### 12. MEASUREMENT AND PAYMENT

#### 12.1 Testing Quantities
- Measured by number of tests performed
- Classification by test type and complexity
- Include mobilization and demobilization
- Separate rates for laboratory and field testing

#### 12.2 Payment Items
- Concrete testing (per m³ tested)
- Soil testing (per test)
- Steel testing (per tonne tested)
- Aggregate testing (per m³ tested)
- Non-destructive testing (per m² tested)
- Laboratory accreditation and maintenance (lump sum)
