# ROAD CONSTRUCTION SPECIFICATION
## Construction Specification Standards - Infrastructure Components

### 1. GENERAL REQUIREMENTS

#### 1.1 Scope
This specification covers the requirements for road construction including sub-base, base course, surfacing, kerbing, and associated infrastructure for the construction project.

#### 1.2 References
- SANS 1200 MK: Road Construction - Materials and Testing
- SANS 1200 MJ: Road Construction - Earthworks
- TMH1: Standard Methods for Testing Road Construction Materials
- COLTO (Committee of Land Transport Officials) Specifications
- TRH 14: Guidelines for Road Construction Materials

### 2. MATERIALS

#### 2.1 Sub-Base Materials
**Material Standards:**
- **G5 Material**: Well-graded gravel material
- **G6 Material**: Poorly graded gravel material
- **G7 Material**: Selected in situ material

**Grading Requirements (G5):**
- Maximum particle size: 37.5mm
- Plasticity index: 6-12
- Liquid limit: 25-35
- CBR (soaked): Minimum 15%

**Chemical Requirements:**
- Sulfate content: <0.5% SO₃
- Organic content: <1% by mass
- pH: 5.5 to 9.0

#### 2.2 Base Course Materials
**Crushed Stone Base (CSB):**
- Los Angeles abrasion: Maximum 30%
- Flakiness index: Maximum 25%
- Aggregate crushing value: Maximum 25%
- 10% fines value: Minimum 100kN

**Grading Requirements:**
- 37.5mm nominal size
- Uniformity coefficient: 1.5-3.0
- Coefficient of curvature: 1.0-3.0

#### 2.3 Surfacing Materials
**Asphalt Concrete (AC):**
- Binder content: 4.5-6.5% by mass
- Marshall stability: Minimum 8kN
- Flow: 2-4mm
- Air voids: 3-5%

**Stone Mastic Asphalt (SMA):**
- Binder content: 5.5-7.5% by mass
- Fibre content: 0.3-0.5% by mass
- Coarse aggregate: 70-80% by mass
- Air voids: 2-4%

#### 2.4 Kerb and Channel Materials
**Concrete Kerbs:**
- Strength: 25 MPa minimum
- Dimensions: 150×250×1000mm (standard)
- Surface finish: Class 3 (fair faced)
- Color: Grey (standard)

**Concrete Channels:**
- Strength: 20 MPa minimum
- Dimensions: 300×150×1000mm (standard)
- Surface finish: Class 2 (ordinary)
- Reinforcement: Y10@200mm centers

### 3. CONSTRUCTION METHODS

#### 3.1 Earthworks Preparation
**Clearing and Grubbing:**
- Remove all vegetation, roots, and topsoil
- Stockpile topsoil for reuse
- Dispose of unsuitable material off-site
- Protect existing services during excavation

**Formation Preparation:**
- Excavate to formation level ±25mm
- Proof roll formation with loaded truck
- Remove soft spots and replace with approved material
- Compact formation to 93% modified AASHTO density

#### 3.2 Layer Construction
**Sub-Base Construction:**
- Spread material in layers not exceeding 150mm loose
- Moisture condition to ±2% optimum
- Compact with smooth drum roller (8-12 tonne)
- Achieve 98% modified AASHTO density

**Base Course Construction:**
- Spread in layers not exceeding 100mm loose
- Maintain uniform moisture content
- Compact with vibratory roller (12-15 tonne)
- Achieve 100% modified AASHTO density

#### 3.3 Surfacing Operations
**Asphalt Mixing:**
- Batch mixing plant with temperature control
- Binder heating to 150-180°C
- Aggregate heating to 160-190°C
- Mix temperature: 140-170°C

**Paving Operations:**
- Paver speed: 3-6 m/min
- Joint overlap: 50-75mm minimum
- Compaction: Within 2 minutes of paving
- Surface tolerance: ±6mm in 3m straightedge

### 4. QUALITY CONTROL

#### 4.1 Layer Thickness Control
**Sub-Base Thickness:**
- Tolerance: ±15mm or 10% of design, whichever greater
- Measurement: Sand cone or nuclear density gauge
- Frequency: Every 50m along centerline

**Base Course Thickness:**
- Tolerance: ±10mm or 8% of design, whichever greater
- Measurement: Core sampling every 500m²
- Acceptance: Within tolerance limits

#### 4.2 Surface Level Control
**Longitudinal Levels:**
- Tolerance: ±15mm in 20m
- Measurement: Precise leveling every 20m
- Adjustment: Within 24 hours of paving

**Cross-Fall and Gradient:**
- Tolerance: ±0.3% of design cross-fall
- Measurement: Digital level every 50m
- Acceptance: Within design parameters

#### 4.3 Surface Regularity
**Straightedge Testing:**
- 3m straightedge on surface
- Maximum deviation: 6mm
- Frequency: Every 100m²
- Correction: Grind high spots, fill low spots

### 5. TESTING PROCEDURES

#### 5.1 Material Testing
**Sieve Analysis:**
- Method: TMH1 Method A1
- Frequency: One test per 500m³ material
- Acceptance: Within grading envelope

**Atterberg Limits:**
- Method: TMH1 Method A2
- Frequency: One test per material source
- Acceptance: Within specification limits

**Compaction Testing:**
- Method: TMH1 Method A7 (modified AASHTO)
- Frequency: One test per material type
- Reporting: Optimum moisture content and maximum dry density

#### 5.2 In Situ Testing
**Density Testing:**
- Method: Nuclear density gauge (ASTM D6938)
- Frequency: One test per 500m²
- Acceptance: 95% of modified AASHTO density minimum

**CBR Testing:**
- Method: TMH1 Method A12
- Frequency: One test per 1000m²
- Acceptance: 80% of design CBR minimum

#### 5.3 Asphalt Testing
**Marshall Testing:**
- Method: TMH1 Method A14
- Frequency: One test per mix design
- Parameters: Stability, flow, air voids, VMA, VFA

**Extraction Testing:**
- Method: TMH1 Method A15
- Frequency: One test per production day
- Acceptance: Within ±0.5% of design binder content

### 6. ROAD PAVEMENT DESIGN THICKNESS

#### 6.1 Flexible Pavement Structure
```
Surface Course:     40mm AC 12.5
Binder Course:      60mm AC 19
Base Course:       150mm Crushed Stone
Sub-Base:          150mm G5 Material
Selected Layer:    150mm G7 Material
Formation:         Natural ground
```

#### 6.2 Traffic Loading Categories
**Category T1:** Light traffic (<10^4 ESA)
**Category T2:** Medium traffic (10^4 - 10^6 ESA)
**Category T3:** Heavy traffic (10^6 - 10^7 ESA)
**Category T4:** Very heavy traffic (>10^7 ESA)

#### 6.3 Design Traffic Loading
- Design life: 20 years
- Growth rate: 4% per annum
- Equivalent Standard Axles (ESA): 10^6
- Pavement category: T2 (medium traffic)

### 7. KERB AND CHANNEL INSTALLATION

#### 7.1 Kerb Installation
**Foundation Preparation:**
- Excavate trench to line and level ±10mm
- Compact base to 93% modified AASHTO density
- Install 50mm bedding sand

**Placement and Alignment:**
- Place kerbs on 1:4 mortar bed
- Align to ±5mm in line and level
- Maintain consistent cross-fall
- Joint width: 3-5mm

#### 7.2 Channel Installation
**Installation Method:**
- Place channels on concrete foundation
- Align with adjacent kerbs
- Maintain gradient for drainage
- Seal joints with flexible sealant

### 8. DRAINAGE REQUIREMENTS

#### 8.1 Surface Drainage
**Cross-Fall Requirements:**
- Carriageway: 2.5% minimum
- Shoulders: 3.0% minimum
- Verge: 4.0% minimum

**Longitudinal Gradient:**
- Minimum: 0.5% for drainage
- Maximum: 8.0% for safety
- Design: 1.0-3.0% typical

#### 8.2 Sub-Surface Drainage
**French Drains:**
- Pipe diameter: 100mm perforated PVC
- Filter material: 19mm crushed stone
- Geotextile: Non-woven, 150g/m²
- Outlet: Connected to stormwater system

### 9. TRAFFIC CONTROL AND SAFETY

#### 9.1 Construction Traffic
**Traffic Management:**
- Maintain one lane open during construction
- Speed limit: 30 km/h through works
- Flagmen at approaches to works
- Warning signs 500m in advance

#### 9.2 Safety Requirements
**Personal Protective Equipment:**
- High visibility vests (Class 2 minimum)
- Safety helmets and boots
- Eye protection for grinding operations
- Hearing protection near noisy equipment

**Equipment Safety:**
- Reverse alarms on mobile equipment
- Backup cameras on trucks
- Emergency stop procedures
- Daily equipment inspections

### 10. ENVIRONMENTAL REQUIREMENTS

#### 10.1 Dust Control
- Water spraying of unpaved surfaces
- Speed limits to reduce dust generation
- Wheel washing facilities at site exits
- Dust monitoring during dry weather

#### 10.2 Noise Control
- Equipment fitted with mufflers
- Operations restricted to 07:00-18:00
- Noise monitoring at site boundaries
- Community notification of noisy activities

#### 10.3 Water Management
- Sediment ponds for runoff water
- Silt fences around disturbed areas
- Temporary drainage during construction
- Protection of watercourses

### 11. MEASUREMENT AND PAYMENT

#### 11.1 Road Construction Quantities
- Measured in square meters (m²) for surfacing
- Measured in cubic meters (m³) for earthworks
- Measured in linear meters (m) for kerbing
- Measured in tonnes for aggregate materials

#### 11.2 Payment Items
- Earthworks (m³)
- Granular materials (m³)
- Asphalt surfacing (m²)
- Kerbing and channeling (m)
- Testing and quality control (lump sum)
- Traffic control (lump sum)
