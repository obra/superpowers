# Incident Investigation Procedure

## Introduction

This procedure establishes the standardized approach for investigating all workplace incidents, accidents, and near misses within [Company Name]. The primary objective is to identify root causes, implement effective corrective actions, and prevent recurrence while ensuring compliance with regulatory requirements and continuous improvement of the Health, Safety, and Environment (HSE) management system.

## Scope of Work

### Purpose
To provide a systematic methodology for investigating incidents that result in injuries, illnesses, property damage, environmental releases, or near misses, ensuring thorough analysis, accurate documentation, and effective preventive measures implementation.

### Responsibilities
- **HSE Manager**: Overall incident investigation program management and oversight
- **Incident Investigation Team**: Conducting detailed incident investigations
- **Supervisors**: Initial incident response and basic investigation coordination
- **Department Managers**: Resource allocation and corrective action implementation
- **Employees**: Participation in investigations and implementation of recommendations

### Timelines
- **Immediate Response**: Within 24 hours for all recordable incidents
- **Preliminary Report**: Within 48 hours for serious incidents
- **Detailed Investigation**: Within 7 days for all recordable incidents
- **Corrective Actions**: Within 30 days for high-priority actions
- **Final Report**: Within 30 days of investigation completion
- **Follow-up Review**: 90 days after corrective action implementation

### Deliverables
- Incident investigation reports with root cause analysis
- Corrective and preventive action plans
- Trend analysis and systemic issue identification
- Training recommendations and updates
- Regulatory reporting and documentation

## Incident Investigation Process

### Incident Classification
- **Fatalities**: Any work-related death
- **Serious Injuries**: Hospitalization, permanent disability, significant injury
- **Recordable Injuries**: Lost time, restricted work, medical treatment beyond first aid
- **Near Misses**: Events with potential for injury or damage but no actual harm
- **Property Damage**: Equipment failure, structural damage, material loss
- **Environmental Incidents**: Spills, releases, regulatory violations
- **Regulatory Incidents**: OSHA recordable, citation-worthy events

### Investigation Levels
- **Level 1 - Supervisor Investigation**: First aid injuries, minor near misses
- **Level 2 - Department Investigation**: Recordable injuries, significant near misses
- **Level 3 - HSE Investigation**: Serious injuries, property damage over $10,000
- **Level 4 - Corporate Investigation**: Fatalities, major environmental incidents

### Investigation Team Composition
- **Team Leader**: HSE professional or designated investigator
- **Operations Representative**: Knowledge of work processes and procedures
- **Maintenance Representative**: Equipment and maintenance expertise
- **Supervisor**: Direct knowledge of personnel and work area
- **Employee Representative**: Worker perspective and experience
- **Specialist**: Technical expertise when required (engineer, safety specialist)

## Immediate Response Actions

### Scene Security
1. **Stop Work**: Immediate cessation of hazardous activities
2. **Isolate Area**: Establish safe boundaries and restrict access
3. **Preserve Evidence**: Protect physical evidence and documentation
4. **Ensure Safety**: Implement measures to prevent additional incidents

### Initial Assessment
1. **Injury Care**: Ensure immediate medical attention for injured persons
2. **Emergency Response**: Coordinate with emergency services if needed
3. **Notification**: Inform management and HSE department promptly
4. **Regulatory Reporting**: Report to appropriate authorities as required

### Evidence Collection
1. **Photography**: Document scene conditions, equipment, and evidence
2. **Witness Identification**: Identify and secure potential witnesses
3. **Physical Evidence**: Collect and preserve relevant physical items
4. **Documentation**: Gather relevant procedures, training records, maintenance logs

## Investigation Methodology

### Information Gathering
1. **Witness Interviews**
   - Conduct interviews as soon as possible
   - Use open-ended questions and active listening
   - Interview witnesses individually to avoid bias
   - Document statements accurately and completely

2. **Document Review**
   - Safety procedures and work instructions
   - Training records and competency documentation
   - Maintenance and inspection records
   - Previous incident reports and corrective actions

3. **Physical Evidence Analysis**
   - Equipment and machinery inspection
   - Environmental condition assessment
   - Personal protective equipment evaluation
   - Work area layout and hazard analysis

### Sequence of Events
1. **Timeline Development**
   - Establish chronological sequence of events
   - Identify key decision points and actions
   - Determine critical time intervals
   - Validate timeline with multiple sources

2. **Causal Factor Identification**
   - Direct causes: Immediate actions or conditions
   - Contributing causes: Factors that influenced direct causes
   - Root causes: Systemic issues underlying the incident
   - Latent conditions: Hidden organizational weaknesses

### Root Cause Analysis

#### Analysis Techniques
1. **Five Whys Method**
   - Ask "why" repeatedly to drill down to root causes
   - Continue until reaching systemic or organizational issues
   - Document each level of questioning and response
   - Validate findings with evidence and logic

2. **Fishbone (Ishikawa) Diagram**
   - Categorize causes into major categories (people, processes, equipment, environment)
   - Identify sub-causes under each category
   - Analyze interactions between different cause categories
   - Focus on systemic rather than individual causes

3. **Barrier Analysis**
   - Identify intended safety barriers and controls
   - Determine which barriers failed or were missing
   - Analyze why barriers were ineffective
   - Recommend barrier improvements

#### Root Cause Categories
- **Human Factors**: Training deficiencies, communication failures, fatigue
- **System Factors**: Inadequate procedures, poor supervision, resource constraints
- **Technical Factors**: Equipment failures, design deficiencies, maintenance issues
- **Organizational Factors**: Policy gaps, cultural issues, management systems weaknesses

## Corrective Action Development

### Action Priority Matrix
- **Immediate Actions**: Implemented within 24-48 hours (high risk, easy fix)
- **Short-term Actions**: Implemented within 30 days (moderate risk, moderate effort)
- **Long-term Actions**: Implemented within 90-180 days (lower risk, significant effort)
- **Continuous Improvement**: Ongoing system enhancements and monitoring

### Action Effectiveness Criteria
- **Specific**: Clearly defined and measurable
- **Achievable**: Realistic and attainable with available resources
- **Relevant**: Directly addresses identified root causes
- **Time-bound**: Clear completion dates and milestones
- **Verifiable**: Measurable outcomes and success indicators

### Action Tracking
- **Action Item Database**: Centralized tracking system for all corrective actions
- **Responsibility Assignment**: Clear ownership and accountability for each action
- **Progress Monitoring**: Regular status updates and milestone tracking
- **Completion Verification**: Evidence-based confirmation of action effectiveness

## Investigation Reporting

### Report Structure
1. **Executive Summary**
   - Brief incident description and key findings
   - Summary of root causes and recommendations
   - Business impact and cost implications

2. **Incident Details**
   - Date, time, location, and circumstances
   - Personnel involved and injuries sustained
   - Property damage and environmental impact
   - Regulatory implications and reporting requirements

3. **Investigation Findings**
   - Sequence of events with supporting evidence
   - Root cause analysis with detailed methodology
   - Systemic issues and organizational factors
   - Comparison with similar incidents or industry trends

4. **Recommendations**
   - Specific corrective actions with priority levels
   - Resource requirements and implementation timelines
   - Responsible parties and accountability assignments
   - Success metrics and verification methods

### Report Distribution
- **Immediate Notification**: Senior management and HSE leadership
- **Regulatory Submission**: Required reports to OSHA, EPA, or other agencies
- **Internal Distribution**: Affected departments, supervisors, and employee representatives
- **Confidential Handling**: Sensitive information shared on need-to-know basis

## Follow-up and Closure

### Action Implementation Monitoring
1. **Regular Status Reviews**
   - Weekly progress updates for high-priority actions
   - Monthly reviews for ongoing corrective actions
   - Quarterly assessment of long-term improvements
   - Annual program evaluation and optimization

2. **Effectiveness Verification**
   - Pre and post-implementation performance metrics
   - Follow-up audits and inspections
   - Employee feedback and perception surveys
   - Incident trend analysis and comparison

### Lessons Learned Integration
1. **Procedure Updates**
   - Revision of affected safety procedures
   - Enhancement of training programs
   - Modification of work instructions
   - Update of emergency response protocols

2. **System Improvements**
   - Management system enhancements
   - Policy and standard updates
   - Technology and equipment upgrades
   - Cultural and behavioral improvements

### Trend Analysis
- **Incident Pattern Recognition**: Identification of recurring themes and issues
- **Risk Assessment Updates**: Modification of risk profiles based on incident data
- **Benchmarking**: Comparison with industry standards and best practices
- **Predictive Analysis**: Use of data to anticipate and prevent future incidents

## Investigation Quality Assurance

### Investigation Standards
- **Thoroughness**: Comprehensive analysis of all relevant factors
- **Objectivity**: Unbiased approach and evidence-based conclusions
- **Timeliness**: Completion within established timeframes
- **Accuracy**: Reliable data and validated findings

### Peer Review Process
- **Independent Review**: Second opinion on major incident investigations
- **Quality Check**: Verification of methodology and conclusions
- **Continuous Improvement**: Feedback for investigation process enhancement
- **Best Practice Sharing**: Dissemination of effective investigation techniques

### Training and Competency
- **Investigator Certification**: Formal training and competency assessment
- **Skill Development**: Ongoing education and professional development
- **Tool Proficiency**: Mastery of investigation techniques and software
- **Cross-training**: Knowledge sharing and backup investigator development

## Record Keeping and Documentation

### Document Retention
- **Investigation Reports**: Minimum 5-year retention period
- **Corrective Action Records**: Until actions verified as effective
- **Photographs and Evidence**: Permanent retention for serious incidents
- **Training Records**: Standard employee record retention policies

### Database Management
- **Incident Tracking System**: Centralized database for all incidents
- **Search and Analysis Capabilities**: Trend identification and reporting
- **Integration**: Linkage with other management systems
- **Security**: Access controls and data protection measures

### Regulatory Compliance
- **OSHA Recording**: Proper classification and recording of work-related incidents
- **EPA Reporting**: Environmental incident notifications and follow-up
- **Worker's Compensation**: Coordination with insurance and claims processing
- **Legal Protection**: Proper documentation for potential litigation defense
