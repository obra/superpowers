# HSE Responsibility Matrix

## Introduction

This document establishes a comprehensive Health, Safety, and Environment (HSE) Responsibility Matrix that clearly defines roles, responsibilities, and accountabilities for HSE management across all levels of [Company Name]. The matrix ensures clarity of expectations and promotes effective coordination of HSE activities throughout the organization.

## Scope of Work

### Purpose
To provide a clear delineation of HSE responsibilities across all organizational levels, functions, and operational areas, ensuring comprehensive coverage of HSE management activities and accountability for performance outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- **HSE Director**: Development and maintenance of the responsibility matrix
- **Human Resources**: Integration of HSE responsibilities into job descriptions
- **Department Heads**: Implementation and communication of role-specific responsibilities
- **All Employees**: Understanding and execution of assigned HSE responsibilities

### Timelines
- Matrix development: Ongoing
- Annual review: Q1 each year
- Updates: As organizational changes occur
- Communication: Immediate upon changes
- Training: Initial upon hire, annual refresher

### Deliverables
- Comprehensive HSE responsibility matrix
- Role-specific HSE responsibility documents
- Integration into organizational charts
- Training and communication materials
- Performance accountability measures

## Responsibility Matrix Framework

### Organizational Structure
- Corporate leadership level
- Division/department level
- Site/facility level
- Supervisory level
- Individual contributor level

### HSE Functional Areas
- Policy development and communication
- Risk assessment and management
- Training and competency development
- Incident investigation and analysis
- Emergency response and preparedness
- Compliance management and auditing
- Performance monitoring and reporting
- Continuous improvement and innovation

### Responsibility Levels
- **Accountable (A)**: Ultimate responsibility and authority for outcomes
- **Responsible (R)**: Primary executor of activities and tasks
- **Consulted (C)**: Provides input and expertise
- **Informed (I)**: Kept aware of decisions and progress
