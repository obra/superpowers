# HSE Information Management

## Introduction

This document establishes [Company Name]'s comprehensive HSE Information Management system as a critical component of the Health, Safety, and Environment (HSE) management framework. The system provides a structured approach to collecting, processing, storing, retrieving, and disseminating HSE-related information to support informed decision-making, regulatory compliance, performance monitoring, and continuous improvement. This comprehensive framework ensures that relevant HSE information is accurate, accessible, timely, and secure while supporting effective communication across all levels of the organization and with external stakeholders.

## Scope of Work

### Purpose
To establish a systematic approach for managing HSE information throughout its lifecycle, from creation and collection through storage, retrieval, sharing, and eventual disposal. The system aims to ensure that decision-makers have access to accurate and timely HSE information, support regulatory compliance and reporting requirements, facilitate effective communication and learning, enable performance monitoring and improvement, and protect sensitive HSE data while maintaining appropriate access controls.

### Responsibilities
- **HSE Manager**: Overall HSE information management system governance and oversight
- **Information Management Coordinator**: Day-to-day administration and system maintenance
- **Department Heads**: Provision of accurate HSE data and information from their areas
- **Supervisors**: Collection and reporting of HSE information from work areas
- **Employees**: Reporting of HSE incidents, hazards, and relevant information
- **IT Department**: Technical support, system maintenance, and cybersecurity protection
- **Legal Department**: Compliance with information privacy and data protection requirements
- **External Auditors**: Independent verification of information management practices
- **Regulatory Authorities**: Recipients of mandatory HSE reporting and disclosure

### Coverage
- All HSE-related information generated, collected, or maintained by the organization
- Incident reports, investigation findings, and corrective action records
- Training records, competency assessments, and certification documentation
- Audit reports, findings, and management review outcomes
- Performance monitoring data and trend analysis reports
- Risk assessment documentation and control measure records
- Emergency response plans and exercise evaluation results
- Contractor safety performance and qualification information
- Environmental monitoring data and compliance reporting
- Occupational health records and exposure monitoring results
- Regulatory correspondence and compliance documentation
- Best practices, lessons learned, and continuous improvement information

### Exclusions
- Personal information not related to HSE functions or workplace safety
- Financial and business information unrelated to HSE management
- Information governed by separate legal or regulatory frameworks
- Classified or proprietary information with special handling requirements
- Personal health information protected under medical privacy laws
- Information related to ongoing legal proceedings or investigations
- Commercially sensitive data unrelated to HSE performance or compliance
