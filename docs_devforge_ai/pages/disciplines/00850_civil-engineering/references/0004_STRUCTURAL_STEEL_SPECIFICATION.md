# STRUCTURAL STEEL SPECIFICATION
## Construction Specification Standards - Structural Elements

### 1. GENERAL REQUIREMENTS

#### 1.1 Scope
This specification covers the requirements for structural steel including beams, columns, connections, and steel fabrication for the construction project.

#### 1.2 References
- SANS 10162-1: Structural Steel - Material and Welding
- SANS 10162-2: Structural Steel - Fabrication and Erection
- SANS 10162-3: Structural Steel - Protective Treatment
- SANS 1431: Welding - Materials and Consumables
- AWS D1.1: Structural Welding Code - Steel

### 2. MATERIALS

#### 2.1 Structural Steel Grades
**Material Standards:**
- **Grade S355JR**: General structural steel (355 MPa yield)
- **Grade S355J2**: Improved weldability (355 MPa yield)
- **Grade S355K2**: Improved toughness (355 MPa yield)
- **Grade S460M/ML**: High-strength steel (460 MPa yield)

**Chemical Composition Limits:**
- Carbon: Maximum 0.20% for S355, 0.16% for S460
- Manganese: 1.60% maximum
- Phosphorus: 0.035% maximum
- Sulfur: 0.035% maximum
- Silicon: 0.60% maximum

#### 2.2 Steel Sections
**Universal Beams (UB):**
- Sizes: 127×76×13 to 1016×305×487
- Grade: S355JR minimum
- Tolerances: ±3mm on dimensions

**Universal Columns (UC):**
- Sizes: 152×152×23 to 356×406×634
- Grade: S355JR minimum
- Tolerances: ±2mm on web/flange thickness

**Hollow Sections:**
- Rectangular: 50×30×2.9 to 500×300×12.5
- Circular: 21.3×2.0 to 508×12.5
- Grade: S355J2H minimum

#### 2.3 Bolting Materials
**High-Strength Structural Bolting:**
- **Grade 8.8:** General purpose (800 MPa tensile)
- **Grade 10.9:** High-strength applications (1000 MPa tensile)
- **Grade 12.9:** Critical connections (1200 MPa tensile)

**Bolt Types:**
- M16, M20, M24, M30 diameters
- Property class 8.8 or 10.9
- Fully threaded or part-threaded

#### 2.4 Welding Consumables
**Electrodes:**
- E7018: General structural welding
- E8018: High-strength applications
- E9018: Critical welded connections

**Welding Wire:**
- G3Si1: Solid wire for MIG welding
- Flux-cored wires for outdoor welding

### 3. CONSTRUCTION METHODS

#### 3.1 Steel Fabrication
**Cutting Methods:**
- Oxy-fuel cutting for thickness >25mm
- Plasma cutting for thickness ≤25mm
- Laser cutting for precision work
- Saw cutting for small sections

**Preparation Requirements:**
- Burr removal from all cut edges
- Surface preparation to grade Sa 2.5 (near white)
- Edge preparation for welding (30° included angle)

#### 3.2 Welding Procedures
**Welding Processes:**
- MMA (Manual Metal Arc) welding
- MIG/MAG (Gas Metal Arc) welding
- FCAW (Flux Cored Arc) welding
- SAW (Submerged Arc) welding for thick sections

**Welding Positions:**
- Flat position (PA): Preferred for quality
- Horizontal-vertical (PB): Acceptable
- Overhead (PE): Limited to non-critical work

#### 3.3 Surface Preparation and Protection
**Blast Cleaning:**
- Method: Abrasive blast cleaning
- Standard: ISO 8501-1 Sa 2.5
- Surface profile: 40-60 microns

**Paint Systems:**
- Primer: Zinc-rich epoxy (75 microns DFT)
- Intermediate: Epoxy mastic (125 microns DFT)
- Finish: Polyurethane (50 microns DFT)
- Total DFT: 250 microns minimum

### 4. QUALITY CONTROL

#### 4.1 Dimensional Control
**Fabrication Tolerances:**
- Length: ±3mm or 1/1000 of length, whichever greater
- Straightness: 1/1000 of length maximum deviation
- Twist: 1mm per meter maximum
- Web/flange squareness: 1mm per 100mm maximum

**Assembly Tolerances:**
- Column plumbness: 1/600 of height
- Beam level: ±5mm in 3m, ±10mm in 6m
- Joint gaps: ±1mm for bolted, ±2mm for welded

#### 4.2 Welding Quality Control
**Visual Inspection:**
- Weld profile and dimensions
- Surface defects (cracks, porosity, undercut)
- Weld reinforcement height
- Throat thickness verification

**Non-Destructive Testing:**
- Magnetic particle inspection (MPI) for surface defects
- Dye penetrant testing (PT) for non-magnetic materials
- Ultrasonic testing (UT) for volumetric inspection
- Radiographic testing (RT) for critical welds

### 5. TESTING PROCEDURES

#### 5.1 Material Testing
**Chemical Analysis:**
- Method: Optical emission spectroscopy
- Frequency: One test per heat number
- Elements tested: C, Mn, P, S, Si, Cu, Ni, Cr, Mo

**Mechanical Properties:**
- Tensile testing: Yield and ultimate strength
- Charpy impact testing: At -20°C for S355, -40°C for S460
- Hardness testing: Vickers or Brinell methods

#### 5.2 Weld Testing
**Destructive Testing:**
- Macro examination of weld cross-sections
- Hardness surveys across weld zones
- Transverse tensile testing
- Bend testing (face and root bends)

**Non-Destructive Testing:**
- Ultrasonic inspection for internal defects
- Radiographic inspection for critical joints
- Magnetic particle inspection for surface cracks

#### 5.3 Coating Testing
**Dry Film Thickness:**
- Method: Magnetic induction gauge
- Frequency: 3 readings per square meter
- Acceptance: ±25 microns of specified thickness

**Adhesion Testing:**
- Method: Cross-cut test (ASTM D3359)
- Acceptance: No loss of adhesion
- Frequency: One test per 100m²

### 6. ERECTION PROCEDURES

#### 6.1 Lifting and Handling
**Lifting Equipment:**
- Mobile cranes with adequate capacity
- Spreader beams for long members
- Lifting lugs designed per SANS 10162-2
- Tag lines for control during lifting

**Storage Requirements:**
- Members stored off ground on timber packers
- Protection from weather and contamination
- Identification tags maintained

#### 6.2 Connection Methods
**Bolted Connections:**
- Hole diameter: 2mm larger than bolt diameter
- Bolt tensioning: Calibrated wrench method
- Torque values per bolt grade and size

**Welded Connections:**
- Site welding limited to non-critical joints
- Pre-qualified welding procedures only
- Welder qualification current and valid

### 8. CORROSION PROTECTION

#### 8.1 Environmental Categories
**Category C1:** Heated buildings with clean air
**Category C2:** Unheated buildings
**Category C3:** Urban and industrial atmospheres
**Category C4:** Industrial areas with high humidity
**Category C5:** Extreme industrial/marine environments

#### 8.2 Coating Systems
**System 1 (C1/C2):**
- Primer: Zinc phosphate (40 microns)
- Undercoat: Epoxy (60 microns)
- Finish: Acrylic (40 microns)
- Total DFT: 140 microns

**System 3 (C3/C4):**
- Primer: Zinc-rich epoxy (75 microns)
- Intermediate: Epoxy (125 microns)
- Finish: Polyurethane (50 microns)
- Total DFT: 250 microns

### 9. MEASUREMENT AND PAYMENT

#### 9.1 Steel Quantities
- Measured by mass (kg) as fabricated and erected
- Include all cutting, drilling, and welding allowances
- Theoretical mass calculations verified by weighing

#### 9.2 Surface Treatment
- Measured by surface area (m²) painted
- Include all surfaces except those specified unpainted
- Shop and site painting included

#### 9.3 Payment Items
- Structural steel fabrication (kg)
- Structural steel erection (kg)
- Surface preparation and painting (m²)
- Welding and bolting (lump sum)
- Testing and inspection (lump sum)
