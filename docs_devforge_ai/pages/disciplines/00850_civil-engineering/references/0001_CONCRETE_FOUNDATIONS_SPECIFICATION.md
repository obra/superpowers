# CONCRETE FOUNDATIONS SPECIFICATION
## Construction Specification Standards - Foundation Systems

### 1. GENERAL REQUIREMENTS

#### 1.1 Scope
This specification covers the requirements for concrete foundations including strip footings, pad footings, raft foundations, and pile caps for the construction project.

#### 1.2 References
- SANS 10100-1: Structural Design - General
- SANS 10100-2: Structural Design - Material and Geotechnical
- SANS 1200 G: Concrete (General)
- SANS 1200 GA: Concrete (Small Works)
- SANS 1200 GB: Concrete (Large Works)

### 2. MATERIALS

#### 2.1 Concrete Mix Design
**Material Standards:**
- **Grade 15 MPa**: General foundations, blinding concrete
- **Grade 20 MPa**: Light structural foundations
- **Grade 25 MPa**: Standard structural foundations
- **Grade 30 MPa**: Heavy structural foundations
- **Grade 35 MPa**: High-strength foundations

**Cement Type:**
- CEM I 42.5N Portland cement
- Maximum cement content: 450 kg/m³
- Minimum cement content: 300 kg/m³

**Aggregates:**
- Coarse aggregate: 19mm nominal size, crushed stone
- Fine aggregate: Natural sand, clean and well-graded
- Maximum chloride content: 0.05% by mass of cement
- Maximum sulfate content: 0.05% by mass of cement

#### 2.2 Reinforcement Steel
**Material Standards:**
- **High tensile steel:** Y500E (500 MPa yield strength)
- **Mild steel:** Y250T (250 MPa yield strength)
- **Surface condition:** Ribbed bars for better bond
- **Diameter range:** 8mm to 32mm

**Protection Requirements:**
- Concrete cover: 50mm minimum for foundations
- 75mm minimum for aggressive soil conditions
- Epoxy coating for chloride environments

#### 2.3 Formwork Materials
- Plywood sheets: 18mm marine plywood
- Timber supports: S7 stress graded pine
- Release agent: Chemically active form oil
- Tie bolts: Galvanized steel, 12mm diameter

### 3. CONSTRUCTION METHODS

#### 3.1 Foundation Excavation
**Method Requirements:**
- Excavate to levels shown on drawings ±25mm
- Maintain verticality tolerance of 1:50
- Remove loose material from base before concreting
- Install blinding concrete (50mm thick, 15 MPa)

**Inspection Requirements:**
- Base level verification before reinforcement
- Soil bearing capacity verification
- Groundwater level monitoring

#### 3.2 Reinforcement Installation
**Placement Tolerances:**
- Bar spacing: ±10mm or 10% of spacing, whichever greater
- Concrete cover: +10mm, -0mm
- Lap lengths: As per SANS 10100-2 requirements

**Quality Control:**
- Bar bending schedules verified on site
- Cover meter checks on completed cages
- Reinforcement fixing certificates maintained

#### 3.3 Concrete Placement
**Placement Method:**
- Pump concrete for foundations >2m deep
- Direct chute placement for shallow foundations
- Maximum free fall: 1.5m
- Vibrate concrete using poker vibrators (25mm diameter)

**Temperature Control:**
- Concrete temperature: 5°C to 32°C at placement
- Cold weather: Use heated aggregates/water
- Hot weather: Use chilled water, cover concrete

### 4. QUALITY CONTROL

#### 4.1 Concrete Testing Procedures
**Slump Testing:**
- Method: SANS 5861-3
- Frequency: One test per 20m³ or per pour, whichever greater
- Acceptance: Within ±25mm of specified slump

**Air Content Testing:**
- Method: Pressure method (SANS 5861-4)
- Frequency: One test per 50m³
- Acceptance: Within ±1.5% of specified air content

**Concrete Temperature:**
- Method: Thermometer in concrete
- Frequency: Each delivery
- Acceptance: 5°C to 32°C

#### 4.2 Strength Testing
**Cube Testing:**
- Method: SANS 5861-1 (150mm cubes)
- Frequency: 3 cubes per 30m³ or per grade change
- Curing: 7 days and 28 days
- Acceptance: Average ≥ specified strength, no cube < 85% of specified

**Non-Destructive Testing:**
- Rebound hammer (optional)
- Ultrasonic pulse velocity (optional)
- Core testing if required

### 5. TESTING PROCEDURES

#### 5.1 Fresh Concrete Testing
**Workability Testing:**
- Slump test every truck
- Flow table test for self-compacting concrete
- Temperature measurement
- Air content verification

**Contamination Testing:**
- Chloride content testing
- Sulfate content testing
- Organic impurities testing

#### 5.2 Hardened Concrete Testing
**Compressive Strength:**
- 150mm cubes at 7, 14, and 28 days
- Core testing (100mm diameter) if required
- Acceptance criteria per SANS 10100-2

**Durability Testing:**
- Water absorption test
- Chloride penetration test
- Carbonation depth measurement

#### 5.3 Reinforcement Testing
**Steel Properties:**
- Yield strength verification
- Tensile strength testing
- Elongation testing
- Bend testing

**Welding Quality:**
- Visual inspection of welds
- Non-destructive testing (dye penetrant, magnetic particle)
- Mechanical testing of weld samples

### 6. CONCRETE MIX PROPORTIONS

#### 6.1 Grade 25 MPa Mix (Typical Foundation Concrete)
```
Cement:        350 kg/m³
Water:         175 kg/m³ (w/c = 0.5)
Coarse Agg:    950 kg/m³ (19mm)
Fine Agg:      750 kg/m³
Admixture:     2.1 kg/m³ (plasticizer)
Air Content:   2.0%
```

#### 6.2 Grade 30 MPa Mix (Heavy Foundations)
```
Cement:        380 kg/m³
Water:         171 kg/m³ (w/c = 0.45)
Coarse Agg:    930 kg/m³ (19mm)
Fine Agg:      740 kg/m³
Admixture:     2.3 kg/m³ (superplasticizer)
Air Content:   1.5%
```

### 7. CONCRETING OPERATIONS

#### 7.1 Weather Limitations
- No concreting in rain
- Wind speed < 20 km/h for exposed surfaces
- Ambient temperature: 5°C to 35°C
- Relative humidity > 30%

#### 7.2 Curing Requirements
**Method 1: Water Curing**
- Keep surface wet for 7 days minimum
- Use wet hessian and plastic sheeting
- Maintain temperature > 5°C

**Method 2: Membrane Curing**
- Apply curing compound within 1 hour
- Coverage rate: 200-300 g/m²
- White pigmented for hot weather

### 8. DEFECTS AND REPAIRS

#### 8.1 Surface Defects
- Blowholes: Grind and patch with repair mortar
- Cracking: Epoxy injection for structural cracks
- Honeycombing: Remove and replace concrete

#### 8.2 Dimensional Defects
- High spots: Grind to level
- Low spots: Patch with repair mortar
- Edge damage: Repair with formwork and new concrete

### 9. MEASUREMENT AND PAYMENT

#### 9.1 Concrete Quantities
- Measured in cubic meters (m³) as placed
- Deduct openings >0.1m²
- No deduction for reinforcement volume

#### 9.2 Reinforcement Quantities
- Measured in kilograms (kg) as fixed
- Include all bars, links, and fabric
- Include cutting/bending allowances

#### 9.3 Payment Items
- Concrete (m³) by grade
- Reinforcement steel (kg)
- Formwork (m²)
- Testing and quality control (lump sum)
