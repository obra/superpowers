# Occupational Health Management

## Introduction

This document establishes [Company Name]'s Occupational Health Management system in accordance with OHSAS 18001/ISO 45001 standards and best practices for occupational health and safety management. The system provides a systematic framework for identifying, assessing, and controlling occupational health risks to protect employee health and well-being. This comprehensive approach demonstrates our commitment to maintaining a healthy workplace, preventing work-related ill health, and continually improving our occupational health performance while supporting business objectives and stakeholder expectations.

## Scope of Work

### Purpose
To establish a comprehensive occupational health management system that identifies occupational health hazards, assesses associated risks, implements effective controls, ensures regulatory compliance, and drives continuous improvement in occupational health performance. The system integrates health considerations into all business decisions and operations to protect employee health, enhance productivity, and minimize health-related business risks while maintaining a positive safety culture.

### Responsibilities
- **Senior Management**: Leadership, commitment, and resource allocation for occupational health
- **Health and Safety Manager**: Occupational health system implementation and maintenance
- **Department Heads**: Occupational health responsibility within their areas of operation
- **Supervisors**: Day-to-day health and safety oversight of their teams
- **Employees**: Compliance with health procedures and active participation in health initiatives
- **Occupational Health Professionals**: Medical surveillance and health risk assessment
- **External Health Specialists**: Independent verification and specialized health services

### Coverage
- All company facilities, operations, and activities globally
- Direct occupational health impacts from company operations
- Indirect health impacts from work organization and psychosocial factors
- Life cycle considerations for processes and work activities
- Emergency situations and their health consequences
- New developments and changes to existing operations
- Outsourced processes that affect employee health
- Temporary workers, contractors, and visitors health protection

### Exclusions
- Personal health conditions unrelated to work activities
- Activities fully regulated by other management systems
- Operations under different legal entities not included in certification scope
- Activities where health impacts are not significant or controllable
- Research and development activities in early experimental phases
- Activities temporarily suspended or not yet operational
- Health impacts from commuting to and from work

## Health Hazards and Risk Assessment

### Hazard Identification Process
1. **Physical Hazards**
   - Noise exposure and hearing conservation
   - Vibration exposure and musculoskeletal disorders
   - Radiation exposure (ionizing and non-ionizing)
   - Temperature extremes and thermal stress
   - Pressure changes and decompression illness
   - Ergonomic stressors and manual handling risks

2. **Chemical Hazards**
   - Toxic substances and poisoning risks
   - Carcinogens and cancer risks
   - Sensitizers and allergic reactions
   - Corrosive materials and chemical burns
   - Asphyxiants and oxygen deficiency risks
   - Dusts and particulates inhalation hazards

3. **Biological Hazards**
   - Infectious agents and disease transmission
   - Allergens and immune system reactions
   - Parasites and vector-borne diseases
   - Biotoxins and natural poisons
   - Genetically modified organisms risks
   - Legionella and water-borne pathogens

4. **Psychosocial Hazards**
   - Work-related stress and mental health impacts
   - Violence and harassment in the workplace
   - Bullying and discrimination effects
   - Work-life balance and fatigue risks
   - Organizational change and uncertainty stress
   - Job insecurity and economic stress impacts

### Risk Assessment Methodology
- **Hazard Analysis**: Systematic identification of potential health hazards
- **Exposure Assessment**: Evaluation of employee exposure levels and duration
- **Health Effect Evaluation**: Analysis of potential health consequences and severity
- **Risk Characterization**: Combination of hazard severity and exposure likelihood
- **Control Hierarchy Application**: Selection of appropriate risk control measures
- **Residual Risk Assessment**: Evaluation of risk remaining after controls implementation

### Significant Health Risks
- **Respiratory Hazards**: Airborne contaminants, dusts, gases, and vapors exposure
- **Dermatological Hazards**: Skin contact with chemicals, biological agents, and irritants
- **Hearing Loss**: Noise-induced hearing impairment prevention
- **Musculoskeletal Disorders**: Ergonomic risk factors and manual handling injuries
- **Occupational Cancer**: Carcinogen exposure and long-term health effects
- **Reproductive Health**: Effects on fertility, pregnancy, and child development
- **Cardiovascular Risks**: Stress, shift work, and physical demands impacts
- **Mental Health**: Work-related stress, depression, and psychological well-being

### Documentation and Review
- **Hazard Register**: Comprehensive inventory of occupational health hazards
- **Risk Assessments**: Detailed analysis of significant health risks
- **Review Schedule**: Regular assessment and update of health risks
- **Change Management**: Health risk assessment for new activities and processes

## Health Objectives and Targets

### Occupational Health Objectives
1. **Hearing Conservation**
   - Reduce noise-induced hearing loss incidents to zero
   - Ensure 100% compliance with hearing protection programs
   - Implement engineering controls for high noise areas
   - Provide annual audiometric testing for exposed workers

2. **Respiratory Protection**
   - Eliminate occupational respiratory diseases
   - Achieve 100% compliance with respiratory protection programs
   - Implement effective ventilation and dust control systems
   - Provide regular respiratory health surveillance

3. **Ergonomic Health**
   - Reduce musculoskeletal disorder incidence by 50%
   - Implement ergonomic assessments for all workstations
   - Provide ergonomic training and equipment
   - Address manual handling risks through job design

4. **Chemical Safety**
   - Eliminate chemical-related occupational diseases
   - Achieve 100% compliance with chemical safety programs
   - Implement substitution of hazardous chemicals where feasible
   - Provide regular health monitoring for chemical exposure

5. **Mental Health and Well-being**
   - Reduce work-related stress and mental health incidents
   - Implement comprehensive employee assistance programs
   - Provide stress management training and resources
   - Promote work-life balance and healthy work environments

### Target Setting Process
- **Baseline Establishment**: Historical health data analysis and current performance assessment
- **Stakeholder Input**: Consideration of regulatory, employee, and business needs
- **Feasibility Assessment**: Technical and financial viability evaluation for health targets
- **Timeline Development**: Realistic scheduling with milestone tracking for health objectives
- **Resource Allocation**: Assignment of personnel, budget, and equipment for health initiatives

### Performance Monitoring
- **Key Performance Indicators**: Measurable metrics for each occupational health objective
- **Regular Reporting**: Monthly, quarterly, and annual health performance updates
- **Trend Analysis**: Identification of health performance patterns and improvement opportunities
- **Benchmarking**: Comparison with industry standards and best practices for occupational health
